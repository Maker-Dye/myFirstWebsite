O L D H I C K O R Y

With the nickname of "Boomtown", Old Hickory was Nashville's original transplant neighborhood. In 1917, as World War I began to take shape, DuPont was granted a government contract to build a factory for gunpowder. The land was developed from farmland to a self-sustaining town for the flood of new residents to the area.

Today, Old Hickory (given from the famous nickname of President Andrew Jackson) is a diverse town that features a variety of local inspiration. Come see what southern living is all about!
